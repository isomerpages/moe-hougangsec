<p><strong>Counselling Support</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>What is Counselling?</strong>
</p>
<p>Counselling provides a safe and supportive environment for students to
share their concerns and work through social, emotional, behavioural, and
mental health challenges with a trained counsellor.</p>
<p>Counsellors support students in developing strategies to manage their
emotions and situations effectively. Through this process, students are
empowered to make informed decisions that support positive growth and well-being.</p>
<p><strong>Support and Referral Process</strong>
</p>
<p>There are three levels of support available:</p>
<p><strong>1. Support by Teachers</strong>
</p>
<p>Students are encouraged to approach their Character Coaches or Year Heads,
who will provide the first level of support and guidance.</p>
<p><strong>2. Referral to Counsellors</strong>
</p>
<p>Students who require more specialised support may be referred to the School
Counsellor or a Teacher Counsellor for early intervention.</p>
<p><strong>3. Referral to Specialists or Community Agencies</strong>
</p>
<p>Students who require more intensive support may be referred to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Lead School Counsellor</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>REACH (Response, Early Intervention and Assessment in Community Mental
Health)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Relevant social service agencies</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Who May Benefit from Counselling?</strong>
</p>
<p>Students may consider seeking support if they experience:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Stress or school-related anxiety</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Academic or examination anxiety</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Grief and loss</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Relationship difficulties with peers</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Family conflicts</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Bullying</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Cyber safety concerns or online threats</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Signs of emotional distress</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>What to Expect During Counselling</strong>
</p>
<p>During counselling sessions, the counsellor will:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Explore the student’s concerns</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Identify appropriate coping strategies</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Build on the student’s strengths and available resources</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Support the development of personalised strategies for well-being</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Confidentiality</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>All counselling sessions are confidential.</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Information shared will not be recorded in report books or academic records
and will not affect future opportunities</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Information will not be disclosed without consent</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Exceptions:<br></strong>Information may be shared if there is
a risk of harm to the student or others, in order to ensure safety.</p>
<p><strong>Parental Consent:<br></strong>All counselling referrals require
parental consent.</p>
